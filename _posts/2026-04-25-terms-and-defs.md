Here are some informal terms and definitions that I'll try to keep up to date.
These are my own working definitions which I try to base on those created by
smarter people than myself, but sometimes feel the need to redefine partially for my own purposes.

## Desired Finite State Machine

The Desired FSM is the FSM that is actually wanted by its creator. It is rare for this machine to be
properly specified- the classic example is trying to shoehorn business logic into the actual application
some developer has to coax into existence.

## Weird states

Weird states are any state that is a discrepancy between the real world emulator
and the desired finite state machine created, usually via software.
